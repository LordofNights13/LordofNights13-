### A bit about me

I have been taking games apart since before I couldn't  read. At four, I figured out how to enter cheat codes in GTA: Vice City by matching the letters on screen one by one. By eleven, I had moved on to Cheat Engine — scanning memory, chasing pointer chains, and patching assembly instructions to bend offline games to my will. That instinct turned into a real skill set over the years, and now I am turning it toward building things instead of just breaking them.

I am especially drawn to open-world games where you can stress-test every system the designers built. The Witcher 3 alone has taken about 350 hours of my life across five full playthroughs — partly because I love the world, and partly because I cannot stop poking at how it works.
